    • Op-ed: WU’s Olympic rings: A ‘spectacular’ commemoration of racism

      • “On Aug. 12 and 13, 1904, the organizers of the Olympic Games partnered with the World’s Fair to host Anthropology Days, a ‘special Olympics,’ in which ‘natives’ would compete alongside their ‘civilized’ American and European counterparts. Chief Organizers William J. McGee and James Sullivan drew the participants from the World’s Fair ethnic displays, which featured ‘savages’ imported from countries around the world.”

        • I learned about the Anthropology Days at the 1904 World's Fair in Dr. Alexis Clark's art history class last semester, and ,as it always is, it was baffling and shocking to see the predominance of racism in every aspect of life back in history. I thought that this was an interesting Op-Ed piece because I wasn't aware of how the “Special Olympics” took place as well. This is a piece of St.Louis history worth knowing, and I hope it makes the WashU community think about it as well.

    • Dozens Of Male Sexual Assault Survivors Share Their Stories, And They’ll Break Your Heart

      • “The pictures feature survivors holding up posters with quotes from their attackers, and many of those who volunteered to be a part of the project were men who had been subjected to sexual assault, child abuse, and domestic violence.”

        • I had never come across this project before, but this series of photographs was extremely sensitive and moving. When people speak of sexual assault, we usually automatically default the victims as females; that's why I think projects like this are very powerful in making people think deeper.
